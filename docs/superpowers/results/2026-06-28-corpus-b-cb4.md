# Corpus (B) CB4 — Acquired-edge arm: daftari derivation vs minting foil

**Date:** 2026-06-28
**Spec:** `docs/superpowers/specs/2026-06-28-corpus-b-cb4-acquired-edge-design.md`
**Models:** both acquirers = `anthropic/claude-haiku-4.5` via OpenRouter, temp 0. One run, ~$1.

**Verdict:** as predicted by the keystone design — daftari's cortex derivation pass
**does not auto-acquire** the stale↔governing relation (recall **1/33**), because
competing versions are a **tension, not a load-bearing derivation**, and it mints
**0** supersessions (structural). The minting foil, forced to assert a direction,
is **position/recency-biased** and **fabricates heavily**: wrong-direction **15/33**
on real pairs and a false supersession on **11/16** unrelated pairs (F=26). The
contrast is the sovereignty argument: auto-minting fabricates rampantly; daftari
refuses to mint and leaves supersession a deliberate act.

## Setup

Run on the 33 scorable stale-trap `(governingText, staleText)` pairs (a real
supersession: governing supersedes stale) + 16 cross-item control pairs (deduped
on `governingNum`; no relation). daftari-way = daftari's **actual** derivation
classifier (vendored verbatim + byte-match drift-guard + the `completeJson`
schema-embedding mechanism). Minting foil = a forced directional supersession
verdict, candidate order randomized by parity.

## Reading

- **daftari-way recall 1/33 is the *predicted* confirmation of design, not a
  failure.** The derivation classifier detects load-bearing *premise* dependency;
  stale-vs-governing are competing *wordings* of one passage — a **tension**, not a
  derivation — so the conservative classifier correctly judges 32/33 "not related."
  The cortex's derivation-first pass therefore does **not** auto-acquire this
  relation (and, since `related=false`, it logs no direction-pending tension from
  this pass either). **Supersession — and even surfacing this conflict — stays a
  deliberate human act**, exactly the keystone. The acquisition gap (15) is the
  honest curation cost, named not hidden.
- **The minting foil fabricates, and isn't even doing real directional reasoning.**
  The per-row pattern is unmistakable: every `gov=A` trial → wrong-direction, every
  `gov=B` trial → correct. The foil almost always asserts "the later-presented
  version supersedes" — a **position/recency bias**. Its 13 "correct" are the parity
  coin-flip aligning with that bias, not skill; on the other half it asserts the
  **wrong** supersession (15/33). And on **11/16 unrelated** pairs it invents a
  supersession that does not exist. This is what auto-minting buys: confident,
  ungrounded supersession verdicts.
- **The sovereignty contrast (the publishable point):** the accumulator move
  (auto-mint the supersession) fabricates on 26 of 49 pairs and is directionally no
  better than recency; daftari's design **mints 0** — it refuses to convert a
  tension into a supersession, surfacing the relation for a human instead.

## Honest precision

- **The randomization mattered.** Without parity-randomizing the foil's candidate
  order, its recency/position bias would have masqueraded as either skill or total
  failure; randomizing revealed it as a coin-flip. (Caught in plan review.)
- ~~**Conservative model → F is a lower bound.** Haiku is cautious; a more aggressive
  model would likely mint *more* on controls.~~ **REFUTED by the model-panel re-run
  (2026-06-29, below): F is model-dependent 6–26/49, and Haiku is near the *high* end —
  the capable model (GPT-4o) abstains and fabricates far *less*. "26/49" was not a lower
  bound.** See the panel section.
- **daftari-way tested the derivation pass, not the tension path.** The cortex's
  tension mechanism (which would be the *right* acquirer for competing-version
  conflicts) is only triggered on `direction-pending` derivations; here `related`
  is false, so it isn't exercised. Whether a tension-detection pass could acquire
  these (still never minting a supersession) is a follow-on — but it would change
  the *acquisition* number, not the never-mint property.
- First valid run was 33/33 unparseable (the acquirer omitted daftari's schema
  embedding); fixed to faithfully reproduce `completeJson`, then re-run.

## Model-panel re-run (2026-06-29)

The original foil ran Haiku only. Re-ran the **same 49 pairs, same parity-randomized
order** across a 3-model panel (Haiku / GLM-4.6 / GPT-4o), abstain-offered foil (the
NEITHER option is available). Only the model varied.

| model | wrong-direction (true) | fabricated (control) | neither (true) | **F** |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Haiku-4.5 | 15/33 | 11/16 | 5/33 | **26/49** |
| GLM-4.6 | 17/33 | 7/16 | 11/33 | **24/49** |
| GPT-4o | 5/33 | 1/16 | 25/33 | **6/49** |

**Reading (a correction, stated straight):**
- **The Haiku-only "26/49" was not a conservative lower bound — it is near the *high*
  end.** GLM-4.6 ≈ Haiku (24); **GPT-4o fabricates far less (6/49)** because, offered the
  abstain option, it takes it (neither 25/33). The earlier "a more aggressive model would
  mint more" prediction is refuted: capability and minting-aggressiveness are orthogonal,
  and the most capable model here is the most restrained.
- **Fabrication in the abstain-offered condition is model-dependent (F = 6–26/49).** So it
  is *not* the robust headline. This mirrors CB6's abstain-offered finding and the Arm B
  softness: a careful, capable, abstain-prompted LLM approximates daftari's restraint.
- **Implication for the paper:** lead the sovereignty contrast with the **forced /
  architectural** condition (CB6: forced masquerade 17/18, near model-independent — a
  consolidation memory that *must* emit one value has no tension slot), and present the
  abstain-offered fabrication (CB4 here, F=6–26/49) as the honest **model-dependent
  softness**. daftari's guarantee is architectural (mints 0 always); the LLM foil's
  fabrication is contingent on model + whether an abstain option is offered.
- daftari-way numbers (recall 1/33, mints 0) are unchanged — daftari's pass stays Anthropic
  and was not part of the panel.
