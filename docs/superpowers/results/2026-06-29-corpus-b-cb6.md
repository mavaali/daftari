# Corpus (B) CB6 — The keystone, measured: tension-masquerade on "no consensus" items

**Date:** 2026-06-29
**Design:** `docs/plans/2026-06-29-two-corpus-sovereignty-paper-design.md` (CB6 section).
**Models:** foil panel = `anthropic/claude-haiku-4.5`, `z-ai/glm-4.6`, `openai/gpt-4o`;
daftari detector = Haiku (Anthropic, faithful to daftari's client); second-rater =
`google/gemini-2.5-flash` (cross-family, independent of all foils). One run, ~$2.

**Verdict:** the keystone — *"a tension may never masquerade as a supersession"* — moves
from *by-construction* to **measured**, on **n=6 genuine tensions across 3 articles**
(Donald Trump, Joe Biden, COVID-19 pandemic). A consolidation memory that must emit a
single current value masquerades a genuine tension as a supersession **17/18 across the
panel** (forced condition: GLM-4.6 6/6, GPT-4o 6/6, Haiku 5/6) — near model-independent.
daftari mints **0/6** and manufactures **0/6** false conflicts. The honest softness: given
an explicit abstain option, the rate is model-dependent (GLM 5/6, Haiku 3/6, GPT-4o 2/6) —
so daftari's guarantee is *architectural*, the LLM's is *contingent on model + an escape
hatch*.

## Setup

The consensus box's **"no consensus" items are genuine tensions**: the status quo holds
*by default*, not by superseding the alternative on the merits — editor-labeled (e.g. Trump
#48 *"…there is no consensus on specific wording, but the status quo is X"*; Biden #2 RfC
closed verbatim *"status=No consensus"*). **Survey finding (2026-06-29):** the
`Talk:.../Current consensus` box is a *rare* institution — of 12 candidate articles only
**Trump, Biden, and COVID-19** maintain a real box with such items. The six currently-active
tensions:

| article | item | tension |
|---|---|---|
| Donald Trump | #15 | quote the popular-vote number, or not |
| Donald Trump | #48 | COVID-response wording (critical vs factual) |
| Donald Trump | #56 | Russian-bounties wording (claim-as-fact vs add "unverified") |
| Donald Trump | #65 | Abraham Accords framing (achievement vs ceremonial) |
| Joe Biden | #2 | include a "gaffes" subsection, or not |
| COVID-19 pandemic | #7 | lead infobox: cases-map vs deaths-map most prominent |

For each, the two positions were distilled from the linked RfC into a pair
`(statusQuo, alternative)` with **ground truth = NEITHER supersedes**, then **gated by a
blind cross-family second-rater** ("is this a genuine unresolved disagreement where neither
has won out?") — **6/6 validated**. No LLM labeler for the ground truth (the editor "no
consensus" close is the truth) → no contamination.

Two foil conditions per model: **forced** (no NEITHER option — models a consolidation
memory whose architecture has no tension slot) and **abstain-offered** (the CB4 foil, which
offers NEITHER — models an LLM consolidation step that *could* decline). daftari's path =
the CB5 contradiction detector (never asks direction → mints 0).

## Reading

- **The keystone, as an architectural fact (forced, 17/18, near model-independent).** A
  memory that must store one current value cannot represent a tension, so it collapses every
  one into a supersession — and this barely depends on model capability (GPT-4o masquerades
  6/6 just as GLM does, when it cannot abstain). The forced verdicts are also
  **position-biased** (mostly the second-presented), same as CB4 — the minter manufactures a
  direction, it doesn't reason one out. *Honest wrinkle:* the forced condition is **not** a
  pure tautology — Haiku *refused* the instruction once (returned "neither" on the COVID
  infobox item despite being told to pick), so a sufficiently conservative model can resist
  even forced. 17/18, not 18/18.
- **daftari preserves both — minting 0 *and* asserting 0 false conflicts.** The detector
  flagged the 3 genuinely oppositional items (Abraham Accords achievement-vs-ceremonial;
  Biden gaffes include-vs-exclude; COVID cases-vs-deaths map) and correctly said NO on the 3
  framing/detail disputes (e.g. "2.5M more votes" and "a larger popular-vote share" are both
  true). This is *not* a daftari miss — it is preserve-not-resolve: daftari neither mints a
  supersession nor manufactures a contradiction where there is only a framing dispute; it
  holds both positions for human resolution (`vault_tension_log`). The guarantee daftari
  makes is **no-mint**, not auto-acquisition.
- **The honest softness (abstain-offered), and what it says about model choice.** With an
  escape hatch, masquerade is model-dependent: **GLM-4.6 is the *most aggressive* minter
  (5/6)**, GPT-4o the most conservative (2/6), Haiku in between (3/6). So "auto-minting
  fabricates" is true of the *architecture* (forced) and of *aggressive* models, while a
  careful, capable, abstain-prompted LLM (GPT-4o) approximates daftari's restraint. daftari's
  edge is that the restraint is **guaranteed by construction**, not contingent on picking the
  right model and offering the right option. (Methodological note: a more capable model is
  *not* uniformly a more conservative foil — GLM, marketed as cheap-but-strong, masquerades
  most; capability and minting-aggressiveness are orthogonal. This is why the foil is a panel.)

## Honest precision

- **n = 6** across 3 articles — the full active "no consensus" yield of every article that
  maintains the box. The box is a rare institution, so this mechanism's ceiling is ~6–8; a
  larger keystone corpus would need broad RfC-close harvesting (a different acquisition,
  losing the clean box label). Stated as a known limitation; the structural guarantee is the
  backbone, the measurement the support.
- **Pairs are distilled, then second-rater-gated.** The status-quo side is grounded in the
  box/RfC; the alternative is distilled from the RfC thread (a judgment step), so the
  second-rater gate (6/6) is load-bearing, not decorative.
- **Single run; LLM nondeterminism is visible** (abstain-offered numbers shift run-to-run at
  temp 0). The forced ~17/18 is stable; abstain-offered should be read as one sample.
- **The forced condition is near-tautological by design** — that is the point (it models an
  architecture); it is reported *with* the abstain-offered condition so it is contextualized.

## For the paper

CB6 is the keystone section of the two-corpus paper: forced 17/18 (near model-independent,
3 articles) is the architectural statement; the abstain-offered range (2/6–5/6) is the honest
softness; daftari's 0-mint / 0-false-conflict is the preserve-not-resolve demonstration.
Combined with the structural guarantee (the loop cannot mint), the spine is no longer
by-construction alone.
