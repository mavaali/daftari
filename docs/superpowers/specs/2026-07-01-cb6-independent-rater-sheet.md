## Instructions (please read fully before starting)

Ten items follow. Each shows two positions, A and B, from a real disagreement about how a
Wikipedia article should read. Your job is to rate each item from the text in front of
you. There are three questions per item:

- **I1 — Distinct:** Are these two positions meaningfully different prescriptions for the
  article? In other words, would the article actually read differently depending on which
  position won? **YES / NO**
- **I2 — Fair:** Is each position stated fairly, as a reasonable view someone in the
  dispute could actually hold, with neither side reading like a strawman or an
  exaggeration? **YES / NO** (if NO, note which side and why in a few words)
- **I3 — Outcome:** From the text alone, can you tell whether the dispute was actually
  resolved in favor of one of these positions? **A WON / B WON / CANNOT TELL**
  ("Cannot tell" is a fully valid answer; do not guess. Only answer A or B if something in
  the text itself tells you who won.)

Rules:

1. Do not look anything up: no Wikipedia, no web search, no asking anyone. Rate only from
   the text on this sheet.
2. Rate all ten items in one sitting, in order.
3. If you happen to already know how one of these real disputes turned out, still answer
   I3 from the text alone, and tick the "prior knowledge" box for that item.
